# SKILL.md — Commercial Law

## name
commercial-law

## description
Full-service commercial contract capability for UK commercial law. Covers drafting, reviewing, negotiating, advising on, and managing precedents for B2B contracts — including contracts for the sale of goods, supply of services, licences, distribution, and framework agreements. Use when working on any commercial contract matter for corporate or business clients in England & Wales.

## When to Use
- Client sends a commercial contract for review (vendor, customer, supply, services, licence)
- Client asks to draft a commercial agreement from scratch or from a precedent
- Client asks for advice on commercial contract terms, risks, or negotiation strategy
- Client asks about enforceability of contract terms or their legal position
- NDA, short-form contract, or commercial letter agreement is needed

## Legal Framework

### Primary Legislation
- **Sale of Goods Act 1979 (SOGA 1979)** — goods contracts; implied conditions as to title (s.12), description (s.13), satisfactory quality (s.14), fitness for purpose (s.14), and sample (s.15). Cannot be excluded in consumer contracts.
- **Supply of Goods and Services Act 1982 (SGSA 1982)** — services contracts; s.13 implies term of reasonable care and skill; s.14 implies services performed within reasonable time where no time specified.
- **Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977 (UCTA 1977)** — exclusion/limitation clauses in B2B contracts. S.2 voids exclusion of liability for death/personal injury from negligence. S.3 applies reasonableness test to standard terms excluding breach liability.
- **Consumer Rights Act 2015 (CRA 2015)** — consumer contracts; unfair terms of no effect; implied terms in goods/digital content contracts. Relevant where B2C elements exist within B2B transaction.
- **Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998** — implies statutory interest at 8% above BoE base rate on qualifying commercial debts. Reference in payment provisions is standard market practice.
- **Misrepresentation Act 1967** — s.2(1) reverses burden of proof for negligent misrepresentation; s.3 requires misrepresentation exclusion to satisfy UCTA reasonableness test.

### Key Case Law

| Case | Principle |
|------|-----------|
| Baird Textile Holdings Ltd v Marks & Spencer plc [2001] EWCA Civ 274 | Long-term commercial relationship does not create binding contract without express terms. Limits of implied terms. |
| Interfoto Picture Library Ltd v Stiletto Visual Programmes Ltd [1989] QB 433 | Onerous/unusual standard terms require reasonable steps to draw to attention. Principle of incorporation. |
| Photo Production Ltd v Securicor Transport Ltd [1980] AC 827 | Clearly worded exclusion clause in B2B contract between equal bargaining parties can exclude fundamental breach. Primacy of freedom of contract. |
| Investors Compensation Scheme Ltd v West Bromwich BS [1998] 1 WLR 896 | Modern contractual interpretation: background knowledge reasonably available to parties at time of contracting. |
| Arnold v Britton [2015] UKSC 36 | Natural meaning of words is starting point; commercial common sense cannot override clear language. Caution on reading down clear terms. |
| Marks and Spencer plc v BNP Paribas [2015] UKSC 72 | Implied terms test: necessary for business efficacy OR so obvious both parties would have agreed. Reasonable and equitable alone insufficient. |
| Cavendish Square Holding BV v Talal El Makdessi; ParkingEye Ltd v Beavis [2015] UKSC 67 | Penalty clause reformulated: secondary obligation penalty only if detriment is disproportionate to legitimate interest. Narrowed penalty doctrine significantly. |
| Triple Point Technology Inc v PTT Public Company Ltd [2021] UKSC 29 | Delay liquidated damages clauses operate up to termination; not extinguished by wrongful termination. |

## Service Line: Drafting

### Tier 1 — Light Draft

**When to use:** Standard NDA, short-form letter agreement, simple services contract, brief licence. Client needs something fast from a precedent with minimal tailoring. Single document. Two parties. Known commercial context.

**Methodology:**
1. Take brief instructions: parties, subject matter, duration, payment, confidentiality, termination, governing law
2. Select appropriate precedent from the firm's library
3. Adapt the precedent: insert client names, key commercial terms, remove irrelevant boilerplate
4. Review for legal accuracy, internal consistency, plain English clarity
5. Deliver with a brief covering note flagging any key provisions

**Quality markers:**
- Document is 2–8 pages
- Immediately understandable to non-lawyer client
- All key commercial terms are present and accurate
- No unnecessary boilerplate
- Covering note flags: definition scope, liability cap, termination, governing law

**Sub-skills:** Precedent selection, clause adaptation, plain English drafting, risk identification (light)

**Legislation:** SOGA 1979 ss.12–15 (for goods contracts), SGSA 1982 s.13 (for services)

**Timing:** 24–48 hours from instruction

**Common pitfalls:**
- Using a US-law NDA for a UK transaction (wrong jurisdiction)
- Failing to tailor the definition of "Confidential Information" to the specific context
- Including irrelevant boilerplate that confuses the client
- Missing governing law and jurisdiction clause
- NDA that is too broad (covers all information without reasonable limitation)

**Escalate if:** Multi-party arrangement, novel contractual mechanism, consumer-facing terms, high value (>£25k), cross-border

---

### Tier 2 — Standard Draft

**When to use:** Fully tailored commercial contract — bespoke services agreement, supply contract, distribution agreement, framework agreement, software licence. Multiple parties possible. Material commercial complexity. Moderate value.

**Methodology:**
1. Confirm all commercial terms with client before drafting: scope, deliverables, payment, IP, liability, termination, consequences of breach
2. Build the agreement from appropriate precedent or from first principles if no precedent fits
3. Draft all material clauses: scope of services/goods, payment and delivery, title and risk, warranty, indemnity, limitation of liability, IP, confidentiality, termination, consequences
4. Apply firm's standard negotiating positions on key commercial terms
5. Include appropriate schedules (definitions, processing schedules if data involved, sub-processors)
6. Internal review against checklist before issuing
7. Deliver with cover note explaining key commercial terms and any areas requiring client decision

**Quality markers:**
- Document is 8–30 pages
- All material commercial terms are clearly and unambiguously expressed
- Structure follows standard commercial contract format
- Schedules are complete and consistent with main body
- Cover note distinguishes: (a) what the firm recommends, (b) what the client must decide, (c) what the firm has assumed

**Sub-skills:** Commercial term analysis, clause drafting, risk allocation, negotiation position calibration, schedule preparation

**Legislation:** SOGA 1979, SGSA 1982, UCTA 1977, CRA 2015, Late Payment Act 1998, Misrepresentation Act 1967

**Timing:** 5–10 working days depending on complexity

**Common pitfalls:**
- Failing to confirm all commercial terms before drafting (leads to need for extensive revision)
- Not addressing IP ownership/licensing in services contracts involving deliverables
- Liability cap set too low relative to contract value (commercially unreasonable)
- Termination provisions inconsistent with payment obligations
- Not flagging limitation of liability exclusions that are commercially aggressive

**Escalate if:** Transaction involves a regulated industry, novel contractual structure, >£100k contract value, multi-party, significant IP assignment

---

### Tier 3 — Bespoke Draft

**When to use:** Novel contractual arrangements, complex multi-party commercial transactions, high-value bespoke agreements, infrastructure or platform contracts, complex IP or data licensing, novel industry arrangements. Strategic importance to client.

**Methodology:**
1. Conduct scoping session with client: understand commercial objectives, not just legal terms; map parties and their respective rights
2. Research any novel legal issues: check for relevant but unusual legislation, no-clear-precedent situations
3. Prepare term sheet / heads of terms before full drafting — confirm commercial agreement
4. Draft from first principles with appropriate precedent as reference only
5. Apply firm's negotiating positions and advise client on their commercial strength
6. Senior solicitor review at draft stage 1 and draft stage 2
7. Prepare negotiation strategy note for client
8. Full quality check: consistency, ambiguity check, cross-references, definitions, schedules
9. Matter opened, file note prepared, conflict check confirmed before drafting commences

**Quality markers:**
- Document of any length (30+ pages for complex matters)
- Comprehensive and coherent; no ambiguity in material provisions
- All parties' rights and obligations clearly mapped
- Risk allocation is commercially rational and legally sound
- Negotiation strategy note accompanies every bespoke draft
- File note records: commercial objectives, key decisions, client's risk appetite

**Sub-skills:** Strategic commercial drafting, multi-party contract architecture, novel legal research, negotiation strategy, senior review coordination

**Legislation:** Full commercial law framework as applicable

**Timing:** 3–6 weeks depending on matter complexity

**Escalate if:** Novel legal issue (always escalate tier 3), client-facing bespoke output, regulated industry, novel AI/technology contract

## Service Line: Reviewing

### Tier 1 — Red Flags Only

**When to use:** Client has received or wants a quick scan of a standard commercial contract. Low risk, straightforward. Client wants to know only if there are serious issues. Budget-conscious client.

**Methodology:**
1. Read the contract quickly — identify the key commercial terms
2. Scan for Tier 1 RED FLAG triggers (see below)
3. If no RED flags: output GREEN with a short note confirming no material concerns
4. If RED flags found: output AMBER or RED with a brief explanation of each flag
5. Recommend client review the flagged items; offer full Tier 2 review if flagged items are material

**RED FLAG triggers (Tier 1):**
- Exclusion of liability for death or personal injury (automatically void under UCTA s.2)
- Uncapped indemnity in favour of the other party
- Termination without notice in a contract with minimum term
- Non-compete or restriction with no time limit or geographic scope
- IP assignment clause where client is providing services (no licence-back)
- Governing law clause applying non-UK law to a UK transaction
- Unilateral variation rights with no notice requirement
- Unlimited liability clause

**Quality markers:**
- Honest and proportionate — identifies material concerns without over-scoping
- Clear explanation in plain English of what each flag means
- Distinguishes between deal-breaker flags and negotiability flags

**Timing:** Same day or 24 hours

**Escalate if:** Any RED flag is found, or if the matter appears more complex than Tier 1 — escalate to Tier 2 full review

---

### Tier 2 — Standard Review with Redline

**When to use:** Client has a material commercial contract and wants a full clause-by-clause review with red-line markup and negotiation recommendations. Moderate complexity. Standard commercial terms.

**Methodology:**
1. Read the entire contract through once before flagging
2. Identify the contract type, the client's side (vendor/customer), and any relevant commercial context
3. Apply the RAG framework (RED/AMBER/GREEN) to each material clause
4. Draft red-line markup with specific suggested amendments for each AMBER and RED clause
5. Prepare a separate negotiation strategy note ranking the issues by priority
6. Flag any clauses that are deal-breakers and any that are acceptable as-is
7. Check: governing law, jurisdiction, termination, IP, liability cap, payment, data processing

**RAG Framework:**

| Rating | Meaning | Action |
|--------|---------|--------|
| GREEN | Clause is balanced, commercially reasonable | No action required |
| AMBER | Clause presents risk/imbalance; flag for negotiation | Note in output; include red-line |
| RED | Clause is one-sided, potentially unenforceable, or material risk | Do not execute without amendment |

**Standard AMBER triggers:** Cap at less than 12 months' fees, notice period less than 30 days, broad IP licence grant, no consequential damages exclusion in client's favour

**Standard RED triggers:** Termination without notice AND non-compete with no time limit, IP assignment with no infringement warranty, uncapped indemnity, exclusion of SOGA/SGSA statutory rights

**Quality markers:**
- Every material clause has a RAG rating with a specific reason
- Every AMBER and RED clause has a specific red-line suggestion
- Negotiation strategy note ranks issues: must-have amendments vs nice-to-have
- Summary note distinguishes: (a) clauses to accept as-is, (b) clauses to negotiate, (c) deal-breakers
- Pricing analysis included if relevant

**Timing:** 3–5 working days for a 20-page contract

**Escalate if:** Multiple RED clauses, novel legal issue, high-value transaction, regulated industry, client is a consumer

---

### Tier 3 — Detailed Review with Negotiation Strategy

**When to use:** Complex commercial contract, significant commercial relationship, multiple parties, high value, novel arrangements. Client needs full negotiation support and strategic advice on their commercial position.

**Methodology:**
1. Full Tier 2 review completed as baseline
2. Analyse the contract holistically — how do clauses interact? What is the overall risk allocation?
3. Prepare a commercial risk report: overall risk profile, most material risks, client's commercial strength
4. Prepare negotiation strategy: client's priorities, acceptable concessions, walk-away points
5. If acting for vendor: ensure firm's standard terms are as pro-vendor as market will bear
6. If acting for customer: ensure firm's standard positions on caps, exclusions, and termination are achieved
7. Prepare position papers for each major negotiation point
8. Senior solicitor review of negotiation strategy before client meeting
9. If client meeting attended: prepare attendance note; follow up with revised mark-up

**Quality markers:**
- Commercial risk report accompanies the review
- Negotiation strategy is specific and actionable — not generic advice
- Senior review documented on file
- File note records: client's commercial objectives, their risk appetite, any constraints on negotiation

**Timing:** 5–10 working days

## Service Line: Negotiating

### Tier 1 — Written Negotiation

**When to use:** Client needs support in a straightforward contract negotiation where correspondence will be managed by email exchange. Standard complexity.

**Methodology:**
1. Obtain clear instructions from client: their objectives, red lines, acceptable terms, walk-away point
2. Prepare negotiation brief: our position, their likely position, gap analysis, agreed terms
3. Review the contract and prepare mark-up
4. Send mark-up or cover letter with client's position
5. Manage responses: relay to client for decision on key issues, handle routine concessions
6. Agree and finalise; instruct to execute

**Quality markers:** Clear instructions on file. Negotiating position documented before correspondence begins. Routine concessions handled without escalating to client unless they breach instructions.

---

### Tier 2 — Negotiation with Calls

**When to use:** Negotiation involving a call or meeting with the other party's solicitors. Standard complexity with material issues requiring real-time decision-making.

**Methodology:**
1. As Tier 1 plus:
2. Prepare call agenda and position papers for each issue
3. Brief client on agenda before call
4. Attend call/meeting with clear mandate
5. Prepare attendance note and circulate to client same day
6. Confirm agreed terms in writing; update mark-up
7. Manage post-call correspondence through to execution

**Quality markers:** File note records all decisions made on call. Mandate was clear before call. Attendance note circulated within 24 hours.

---

### Tier 3 — Full Deal Management

**When to use:** Complex multi-issue negotiation with significant commercial stakes. Multiple parties, senior stakeholders, or material legal complexity. Negotiation strategy is central to the engagement.

**Methodology:**
1. Appoint lead negotiator; confirm mandate and authority with client
2. Prepare comprehensive negotiation strategy: issues, positions, BATNAs, sequencing
3. Prepare position papers for each party
4. Coordinate with client's commercial team and other advisors
5. Lead negotiation sessions; manage dynamics between parties
6. Record all agreements and partial agreements in real time
7. Prepare heads of terms following successful negotiation
8. Instruct transaction team to complete documentation
9. Report to client with implementation timeline

## Service Line: Advising

### Tier 1 — Quick-Turnaround Note

**When to use:** Client has a discrete question about a commercial contract term, their legal position, or a specific issue. Answer is known or can be established quickly. Straightforward question.

**Methodology:**
1. Clarify the question if not fully understood
2. Identify applicable legal framework and any relevant precedents
3. Draft concise advice note: issue, legal analysis, conclusion, recommendation
4. Check advice against SRA requirements for written advice
5. Issue to client

**Quality markers:** Concise and direct. Answers the specific question asked. Distinguishes legal position from commercial recommendation. Flags any limitations or qualifications.

**Timing:** 24–48 hours for a discrete query

---

### Tier 2 — Detailed Legal Opinion

**When to use:** Complex question requiring substantive legal analysis, research, and a detailed written opinion. Legal position may be uncertain or involve competing interpretations.

**Methodology:**
1. Clarify scope and confirm instructions in writing
2. Research: legislation, case law, regulatory guidance, market practice
3. Prepare detailed opinion: issue, facts, law, analysis, conclusions, recommendations
4. Senior review before issuing
5. Advise client of any limitations of the opinion or areas where law is uncertain

**Quality markers:** Comprehensive analysis of relevant law. Conclusions are clearly reasoned and supported. Caveats and limitations are explicit. Practical recommendations are included.

**Timing:** 5–10 working days

---

### Tier 3 — Ongoing Advisory Retainer

**When to use:** Client requires regular commercial legal advice as their business develops. High-value client or complex commercial arrangements. Strategic advisory relationship.

**Methodology:**
1. Confirm scope, fee structure, and escalation arrangements in engagement letter
2. Assign relationship partner with agreed response time commitments
3. All advice reviewed by partner before issue
4. Maintain matter file with running log of advice given and commercial context
5. Proactive contact when relevant new legislation or case law affects client's sector
6. Quarterly review meeting (or as agreed)

---

## Service Line: Precedent & Process

### Tier 1 — Single Template

**When to use:** Client needs a standard document adapted for their use. Simple matter. No bespoke drafting required.

**Methodology:**
1. Identify appropriate precedent from library
2. Tailor to client's specific context: names, dates, commercial terms, jurisdiction
3. Advise client on key provisions and any unusual terms

**Timing:** 24–48 hours

---

### Tier 2 — Template Suite

**When to use:** Client needs a suite of related commercial documents (e.g., suite of NDA + services agreement + data processing agreement). Standard commercial arrangements.

**Methodology:**
1. Map the document suite required
2. Select appropriate precedents for each document in the suite
3. Ensure consistency across documents: definitions, cross-references, effective dates, expiry
4. Prepare suite with cover note explaining each document's purpose and when to use
5. Advise on order of execution

**Timing:** 5–10 working days

---

### Tier 3 — Playbook

**When to use:** Client needs a comprehensive negotiation playbook governing a commercial relationship or category of contracts. Complex or long-term commercial relationship.

**Methodology:**
1. Scoping session: map all contract types in the relationship, all negotiation scenarios, all stakeholders
2. Research applicable legislation, regulatory requirements, and market practice for each contract type
3. Draft playbook: policy positions, red lines, acceptable variations, escalation procedures, execution process
4. Prepare template documents for each contract type
5. Senior review and client sign-off on policy positions before playbook is issued
6. Training session for client's commercial team (if appropriate)

---

## Universal Escalation Triggers

Escalate to AD-Review (human solicitor) in all cases where:
- Any RED clause in a reviewing engagement where client intends to execute without amendment
- Any matter where >£50,000 is at stake
- Novel legal issue with no clear precedent
- Multi-party arrangement with significant complexity
- Any regulatory element (financial promotions, consumer legislation, data protection authority involvement)
- Client expresses dissatisfaction with AI-assisted process
- Conflict of interest detected
- Matter involves potential litigation
