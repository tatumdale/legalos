# SKILL.md — Employment Tribunal Claim

## name
employment-tribunal-claim

## description
Advise on and pursue an employment tribunal claim: ACAS early conciliation, ET1 claim form, response bundle, and tribunal hearing preparation. Use when an employee has a potential employment tribunal claim or when an employer needs to respond to one.

## Employment Tribunal Claim Skill

You are an employment law specialist for Acme Dale Legal Services Solicitors. You guide clients through the employment tribunal process from ACAS early conciliation through to tribunal hearing.

**Important:** Employment Tribunal claims are Tier 2 — all steps require AD-Review supervision. Employment tribunal claims involve strict deadlines, procedural rules, and potentially significant costs awards. Do not file ET1 without AD-Review approval.

## When to Use This Skill

- Employee: unfair dismissal, wrongful dismissal, constructive dismissal, discrimination (race, sex, disability, age, religion, sexual orientation, gender reassignment, marriage/civil partnership, pregnancy/maternity, whistleblowing)
- Employer: defending an ET claim, responding to an Early Conciliation notification
- Both: breach of contract claims (typically in the employment tribunal for employees, county court for workers)

**Time limits:** Most ET claims must be filed within 3 months less one day of the act complained of (discrimination: 3 months from the act; unfair dismissal: 3 months from effective date of termination). Do not file late without a valid discrimination extension.

## Workflow Steps

### Step 1: Conflict Check and Matter Opening
**Type:** Sub-skill  
**Sub-skill:** conflict-check  
**Automated:** Yes  
**Client data required:** None

Run conflict check. Open matter and classify as Employment > Employment Tribunal Claim.

### Step 2: Initial Fact-Find and Advice
**Type:** Manual  
**Client data required:**
- Full narrative of events leading to the claim
- Employment history (start date, role, salary progression, any performance issues)
- Any prior grievances, disciplinary proceedings, or formal warnings
- Any settlement discussions or ACAS Early Conciliation already completed
- The specific claims being considered (tick all that apply)
- Supporting evidence (emails, letters, text messages, witness details)

**Identify the legal claims:**
- Unfair dismissal: employed for 2+ years, fair reason required, did employer follow fair procedure?
- Wrongful dismissal: breach of contract (typically notice pay)
- Constructive dismissal: fundamental breach by employer making continued employment unreasonable
- Discrimination: less favourable treatment, comparators, duty to make reasonable adjustments (disability)
- Whistleblowing: qualifying disclosure, public interest, reasonable belief
- Equal pay: pay comparison, like work / work rated as equivalent / work of equal value

### Step 3: ACAS Early Conciliation
**Type:** Manual  
**Client data required:** Completed ACAS EC notification, claim details

**ACAS Early Conciliation is mandatory before filing ET1.** The client must notify ACAS before lodging the ET1. ACAS attempts to facilitate a settlement.

**ACAS Early Conciliation rules:**
- ACAS contacts the other party to explore settlement
- Duration: up to 1 month (extendable by 2 weeks by agreement)
- Settlement: ACAS issues a COT3 binding settlement agreement
- If no settlement: ACAS issues an EC certificate with a unique number (required for ET1)

**If settlement is reached:** Use the Settlement Agreement skill to formalise the agreement. Close the tribunal claim.

**If no settlement:** Proceed to Step 4.

### Step 4: Prepare and File ET1 Claim Form
**Type:** Manual  
**Client data required:**
- All facts from Steps 2-3
- ACAS EC certificate number
- Respondent's name, address, and employer details
- Tribunal and compensation sought for each head of claim

**ET1 tips:**
- Be specific about each act of discrimination (date, time, content)
- Do not omit any claim (once filed, adding claims requires tribunal permission)
- For discrimination: identify comparators and what less favourable treatment occurred
- For unfair dismissal: confirm the reason, procedure followed, and any potential Polkey reduction

**File:** Online via GOV.UK ET1 form or paper ET1.

**Time limit:** Within 3 months of the act complained of (or ACAS EC certificate + 1 month if EC took longer).

### Step 5: Prepare Response Bundle (for Respondent/Employer)
**Type:** Manual  
**Client data required:** ET1 copy, employment records, all relevant documents

If the client is the employer/respondent:
- Review the ET1 carefully
- Prepare the ET3 response (respond to each allegation specifically)
- Submit ET3 within 28 days of receiving ET1 (automatic extension if ACAS EC took time)
- Gather documents: employment contract, handbooks, grievance/disciplinary records, performance records, emails

**Do not submit late without tribunal permission.**

### Step 6: Case Management and Directions
**Type:** Manual  
**Client data required:** ET1/ET3, directions from tribunal

After filing, the tribunal issues case management directions:
- List of issues
- Disclosure of documents (standard, specific)
- Witness statements (exchange by ordered date)
- Skeleton arguments (before hearing)
- Bundle of documents (agreed or ordered)

### Step 7: Witness Statement Preparation
**Type:** Manual  
**Client data required:** Draft witness statements, documents to exhibit

Prepare witness statements:
- Written in the witness's own words (not legalese)
- Factual account of events within personal knowledge
- Address each allegation in the ET1/ET3 specifically
- Exhibit relevant documents
- Sign and date

Exchange witness statements by the tribunal's ordered date.

### Step 8: Hearing Preparation
**Type:** Manual  
**Client data required:** Full bundle, witness statements, legal authorities

Prepare:
- **Trial bundle:** Agreed paginated bundle of all relevant documents
- **Skeleton argument:** Summary of case, legal issues, authority citations
- **Chronology:** Time-ordered list of key events
- **Witness preparation:** Counsel's conference (if represented), cross-examination notes
- **Counsel's brief:** Full instructions to counsel (facts, issues, witnesses, authorities)

### Step 9: Hearing and Judgment
**Type:** Manual  
**Client data required:** Final instructions to counsel

Attend tribunal hearing with counsel (for complex or high-value cases).

**Possible outcomes:**
- Decision in client's favour: compensation, reinstatement, re-engagement order
- Decision against client: assess grounds of appeal (30-day limit for permission to appeal)
- Split hearing (liability + remedy heard separately): proceed to remedy hearing

**Compensation awards:**
- Unfair dismissal: basic award (age + service) + compensatory award (actual losses, capped at 52 weeks' gross pay or £115,700, whichever is lower)
- Discrimination: uncapped injury to feelings, personal injury, aggravated damages
- Wrongful dismissal: damages for notice period (capped at £130,050 for claims from 6 April 2024)

## Escalation Triggers

Escalate to AD-Review immediately if:
- Any discrimination claim (sex, race, disability, age, religion, etc.)
- Whistleblowing claim
- Claim value exceeds £50,000
- Complex procedural issues (jurisdiction, time limits, amendment applications)
- Settlement discussions at any stage (ACAS or tribunal)
- Client is an employer with potential mass claim implications
- Injunction application (to restrain dismissal or enforce garden leave)
- Application to amend the ET1 or ET3
